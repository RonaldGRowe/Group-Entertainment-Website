Role-Playing Games

Role-playing games (RPGs) are games that allow you to create your own character and enter a world where you have the right to choose your character's actions, traits, morals, and other characteristics. Everything single that occurs in the game is a result of the player's choices and actions. This gives the player freedom to progress in the game however they choose. This also gives the player the ability to explore the world that exists inside of this game. It is a sub-reality for the player.

Survival Horror

Survival Horror is a genre that gives the player the sense of being part of a horror film. It thrives off of the fears of the player, and subjects the player to terrifying scenes that would create great fear in real life. Depending on the player's reaction, the player may not survive to see the next scene. These games are not recommended for small children or those who are easily startled. It is unlikely that a fearful person will survive long in these games. Survival horror games prepare players to survive their worst nightmares.

Platformer

Platform games are side-scrolling, which simply means that the game's background passes along behind the character, and the character is only allowed to move forward (which is usually toward the right). Many platform games have become very popular and have out-lasted the advances in gaming technology. Mario, Sonic the HedgeHog, and Donkey Kong are some of the original platform games that are still among players' favorites. Most platform games are single player.

Fighter

Fighting games give players a means of competitiveness that involves virtual hand-to-hand combat. These games may be based on wrestling matches, boxing matches, or even be set in a war zone. Many of these games include characters who possess special abilities and powers that can be used against opponents to give the player an edge of advantage. Although these games can be played by a single player against the game itself, they are more challenging when played against another person.

First-Person Shooter

First-person shooter games test a player's attention to detail, reflexes, and ability to react to situations quickly and effectively. Successful players are able to quickly assess a situation and react to it appropriately. The player's perspective of sight in these games is realistic in the sense that the player's character sees down the barrel of the weapon they are holding. Players are usually given an idea of direction by a mini-map that is normally near the bottom of the screen.
