Fishing

For some people,fishing is a competitive sport, where each fisherman is vying for the largest or rarest catch. For others, it is a way of life, providing the thousands of pounds of fish available in the restuarants, grocery stores, and fish-marts nationwide. But for most fishermen, it is simply a relaxing, peaceful way to spend their time away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. It provides a time of reflection and relaxation that no other sport comes close to.

Hunting

Hunting has not always been considered a "sport." In the beginning times, hunting was a necessity; a means of providing food for one's self and family. Without this, people would have never known the taste of meat. Today, hunting is still a means of providing food, but is not as much of a necessity since meat can simply be purchased. Although the meat in the market did not gallop in from the forest. Someone hunted it. Hunting is now done moreso for the feeling of accomplishment it provides to the hunter.

Water Sports

Water sports are growing in popularity as people realize that we are nearly limitless in what we can do if given enough water and imagination. Water sports include swimming, water-skiing, jet-skiing, kayaking, sailing, surfing, rowing, rafting, parasailing, and much much more. Water sports provide a fun variety of exercise and adventure. Whether self-propelling, being hauled by a boat, or dropped from a plane, if water is involved, the sport is most likely going to be awesome!

Snow Sports

Snow sports are similar to water sports; except that in these sports, the water is frozen! There are many snow sports, some more popular than others. For example, everyone has heard of snow skiing, snowboarding, speed skating, and figure skating. Not as popular, but just as exciting (if not moreso) are: bobsledding, luging, and skeletoning. Whether you prefer style and grace, or daredevil excitement, there is a snow sport that fits your personality perfectly.
